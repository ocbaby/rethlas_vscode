# theorem thm:prime_order_group_cyclic

## statement
Prove that every finite group of prime order is cyclic.

## proof
Let $G$ be a finite group of prime order. Write $|G|=p$, where $p$ is prime.

Choose an element $g\in G$ with $g\neq e$, where $e$ is the identity of $G$. Consider the cyclic subgroup $\langle g\rangle$ generated by $g$. Its order is the order of the element $g$, so it is a positive divisor of $|G|=p$. Since $g\neq e$, the element $g$ has order greater than $1$. Because the only positive divisors of the prime number $p$ are $1$ and $p$, it follows that
$$
|\langle g\rangle|=p.
$$

Therefore $\langle g\rangle$ has the same number of elements as $G$. Since $\langle g\rangle\leq G$, we must have
$$
\langle g\rangle = G.
$$

Thus $G$ is generated by $g$, and hence $G$ is cyclic.
