Prove the following theorem.

Let $K \cong T^n$ be a finite nonabelian characteristically simple group, where $T$ is a nonabelian simple group and $n\ge 1$. Let $\Gamma = \mathrm{Cay}(K,S)$ be a connected tetravalent half-arc-transitive Cayley graph. If $T$ is not isomorphic to any of: 
$A_{2m-1}$ for $m\ge 3$; $\mathrm{PSL}_4(2)$, $\mathrm{Suz}$; $\mathrm{PSL}_m(2^e)$, $\mathrm{PSU}_m(2^e)$ for $m\ge 4$;\; $\mathrm{PSp}_m(2^e)$ for $m\ge 6$;\; $E_6(2^e)$, $E_7(2^e)$, ${}^2E_6(2^e)$, ${}^2G_2(2^e)$;
then $\Gamma$ is a normal Cayley graph, i.e., $\mathrm{Aut}(\Gamma) = K\rtimes\mathrm{Aut}(G,S)$.
